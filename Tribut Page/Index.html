Here's a time line of Atal Bihari Vajpayee:

  • 1924 - Born in Gwalior, Madhya Pradesh
  • 1934 - Vajpayee did his schooling at the Saraswati Shishu Mandir in Gwalior
  • 1939 - He also joined the Rashtriya Swayamsevak Sangh (RSS) in as a swayamsevak, or volunteer.
  • 1944 - His activism started in Gwalior with Arya Kumar Sabha, the youth wing of the Arya Samaj movement, of which he became the general secretary.
  • 1979 - In 1979, Desai and Vajpayee resigned, triggering the collapse of the Janata Party.
  • 1980 - The erstwhile members of the Bharatiya Jana Sangh came together to form the Bharatiya Janata Party (BJP) in 1980, with Vajpayee as its first President
  • 1996 - (First Vajpayee ministry) During a BJP conference in Mumbai in November 1995, BJP President Advani declared that Vajpayee would be the party's prime ministerial candidate in the forthcoming elections.Vajpayee was sworn in as the 10th prime minister of India,but the BJP failed to muster a majority among members of the Lok Sabha. Vajpayee resigned after 16 days, when it became clear that he did not have enough support to form a government.
  • 1998-1999 - (second vajpayee ministry) The 1998 general elections again put the BJP ahead of others. A number of political parties joined the BJP to form the National Democratic Alliance (NDA), and Vajpayee was sworn in as the prime minister.
  • 1999-2004 - ( third vajpayee ministry) Vajpayee took oath as the prime minister of India for the third time.
  • 2001 - the Vajpayee government launched the Sarva Shiksha Abhiyan campaign, aimed at improving the quality of education in primary and secondary schools.
  • 2002 - The Vajpayee administration brought in the Prevention of Terrorism Act in 2002. The act was aimed at curbing terrorist threats by strengthening powers of government authorities to investigate and act against suspects.It was passed in a joint session of the parliament, amidst concerns that the law would be misused.
  • 2005 - Vajpayee announced his retirement from active politics, declaring that he would not contest in the next general election.
  • 2009 - Vajpayee was hospitalised at All India Institute of Medical Sciences, Delhi (AIIMS) for a chest infection and fever on 6 February 2009. He was put on ventilator support as his condition worsened but he eventually recuperated and was later discharged.
  • 2018 - dies at the age of 93.
